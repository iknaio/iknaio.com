The [Bank of International Settlements (BIS)](https://bis.org/) and its Eurosystem partners released a [report](https://www.bis.org/publ/othp76.htm) on initial findings from [Project ATLAS](https://www.bis.org/about/bisih/topics/suptech_regtech/atlas.htm). This initiative examines international cryptoasset flows by merging exchange data with blockchain transaction information.

A key insight emerged from the analysis: "Even though blockchain data are openly available, methodologies for data aggregation are often a black box, preventing researchers from engaging in deep-dive analysis and regulators from benchmarking data across sources"

Iknaio enthusiastically backs this effort. Committed to openness and accountability, the company prioritizes open-source development and rigorous documentation of data origins. This approach ensures universal access to software tools and full traceability of processed information to its source. The organization aims to empower clients with reliable, verifiable data to inform their strategic choices.
